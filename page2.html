Academic Life as a Midshipman

Midshipman are held to high standard as future Naval Officers. All midshipman must maintain a 2.5 GPA each semester or face academic probabtion. The midshipman recieve guidance from their class advisors, and are mentored each step of the way. Navy Option students are required to take math and physics reguardless of their major to be better prepared to join the fleet. Marine Option students are required to take extra history and english courses to prepare for their deployments to foreign nations. Our midshipman are studing a wide range of topics, so no matter what your passion is you'll find a friend and a mentor at UNC!

Midshipmen take the same basic naval science courses and laboratories in the first two years. This involves a combination of courses in naval science and the Curriculum in Peace, War, and Defense designed to provide a well-rounded individual to commissioned service. During the junior and senior years, students who desire a commission in the Marine Corps take NAVS 311 and 411 in place of NAVS 202, 301, 302, and 401.

Military Training as a Midshipman

Once a week, Navy and Marine-Option midshipmen participate in a two and half hour lab during which they receive the majority of their military instruction. Held in the Naval Armory, lab is a time to receive necessary training, participate in hands-on learning, and practice their leadership skills. Lab activities are coordinated efforts between the Active Duty Staff and the midshipmen Battalion Staff. Activities include briefs, breakout sessions, tactical training scenarios, drill and formation practice, sword and weapons handling, leadership reaction courses, and warrior training. Various speakers will also attend the weekly lab, including Active Duty and Retired Naval Officers and Enlisted Personnel, historians, EMTs, and other professionals offering young midshipmen the opportunity to interact with seasoned leaders in their respective fields.

Throughout the week, midshipmen participate in endurance, speed, and strength workouts during morning and afternoon PT sessions. These sessions are designed specifically with the goal of improving the midshipmanâ€™s performance on the Navy or Marine Corps PT test. With that being said, the number of PT sessions a midshipman must attend in a week is based on his/her performance on their respective PT test. Once a week, the entire battalion forms for a large PT session. These sessions can include anything from rigorous calisthenics, to a motivating formation run through campus, to a squad competition where the best squad wins bragging rights, at least until the next competition.
